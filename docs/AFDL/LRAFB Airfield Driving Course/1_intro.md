# Lesson 1: Introduction

Welcome to the Airfield Driving Training and Certification System (ADTCS). 

There are two courses available, and your license will show restrictions as either Controlled Movement Area (CMA) or non-CMA. Ensure you have selected the appropriate training prior to moving forward. Licensing will be completed through this site. While you can review training material independently, Attachment 7 must be completed with your trainer or unit ADPM.

Both the trainee and trainer must provide digital signatures to confirm certification. **NOTE**: Attachment 7 does not need to be maintained separately, as it will remain on this platform.

If you have not been briefed to drive on the or access the Christmas Tree apron, you do not require CMA access and will be classified as non-CMA. The Wing ADPM is the final authority for members being licensed as CMA.

For those of you who require CMA access here are the color vision requirements from DAFI 13-213, Airfield Driving:

**Color Vision Requirements**. Individuals that have a requirement to drive a vehicle on the CMA must pass a color vision test. (T-1). Contact the base hospital or medical treatment facility Optometrist for assistance in determining the best process for administering the color vision test to drivers that operate a vehicle on the CMA.

: Individuals that fail to pass the color vision test can be issued a "ramp only" AF Form 483. **NOTE**: Access to the CMA must not be granted. (T-1).

: Individuals that have an Air Force Specialty Code with a mandatory requirement for normal color vision are exempt from color vision test.

: Unit ADPMs can use the Officer and Enlisted Classification Directory for Air Force Specialty Codes ![https://www.afpc.af.mil/Classification/](https://www.afpc.af.mil/Classification/) to check vision requirements. Both products are available for viewing and download on the Air Force Portal.
