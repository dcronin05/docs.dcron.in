AND/OR NON-VEHICLE EQUIPMENT ON THE

## Vehicle requirements
All vehicles operating on the airfield must contain:

* 2-way radio (If operating within the CMA)
* DAFVA 11-240, *USAF Airport Signs and Markings*
* DAFVA 13-222, *Runway/ Controlled Movement Area Procedures*
* Most up to date Airfield Diagram 
* FOD Can (Foreign Object Damage)

## Towing Operations
Towing speed is 5 MPH for all aircraft and two or more 
maintenance stands. Towing speed for one maintenance stand is 
10 MPH. Towing speed for AGE such as compressors ground power 
units, air start carts, and similar equipment is 15 MPH.

## Speed Limits

* Flight line Road: 15 MPH    
* Vehicle Parking Areas: 5 MPH
* Perimeter Roads: 15 MPH
* Aircraft Parking Ramp or within 200ft of area: 15 MPH (Note: within 50 feet of 
any aircraft: 5 MPH)
* Taxiways: 15 MPH (within 25 feet of any aircraft: 5 MPH)
* Runway/Assault Zone: 30 MPH
* During reduced visibility, snow, or ice conditions: 10 MPH  

## Parking and Chocking Requirements 

Do Not Park Vehicles or Equipment Closer Than:

* 1,000 feet from Runway centerline
* 200 feet from Taxiway centerline
* 125 feet from edge of Apron
* 25 feet of any aircraft, unless fueling or servicing the aircraft

## Parking and Chocking Requirements

1. **NEVER**{.important} leave a vehicle unattended on the Runway, Assault 
Zone, or Taxiways.
2. Vehicles left unattended on the parking apron must 
adhere to the following:
      * Vehicles will be parked as to not interfere with aircraft 
      being towed or taxiing. 
      * Engine will be turned off and keys left in the ignition.
      * The transmission will be placed in park (automatic) or 
      reverse (manual).
      * Parking or Emergency brake will be set. Vehicles that do 
      not have an internal braking system will have chocks 
      placed in front of and behind one of the rear wheels.
      * Vehicle doors will remain unlocked.

## Operating Vehicles in the Vicinity of Aircraft

* Aircraft **ALWAYS**{.important} have the right of way.   
*  **DO NOT**{.important} drive between aircraft and the "Follow Me" vehicle.
*  **DO NOT**{.important} drive on taxiway shoulders (Outside the lights). 
* No vehicle will park or pass closer than 25 feet in front of or 200 feet to the 
rear of an aircraft that has its engines running.
* When approaching a parked aircraft always approach with the driver's side of 
the vehicle towards the aircraft.
* Always drive on the right hand.
* Do not operate vehicles within 100 feet of a helicopter with rotors in motion.

## Runway Incursions

![]\(../../files/imgs/training_slides_page_10_2.png)
Runway incursion occurs when an aircraft, vehicle or person 
enters the protected area of a surface designated for the 
landing and take-off of aircraft without approval from Air Traffic 
Control Tower. The leading causes of these incidents result 
from a failure to follow procedures, inadequate vehicle 
operator training, and loss of situational awareness. 

## Airfield Markings/Signs

![]\(../../files/imgs/training_slides_page_11_1.png)

### Instrument Hold line (ILS)

Red background with white letters "INST". These 
signs are located adjacent to all Runway Hold line 
markings. **NEVER GO PAST THESE SIGNS WITHOUT 
THE CONTROL TOWER APPROVAL WHEN 
INTERNALLY LIT AND/OR THE CONTROL TOWER 
HAS INSTRUCTED THAT PRECISION  APPROACH 
CRITICAL AREA RESTRICTIONS ARE IN EFFECT.**{.important}

### Instrument Hold Position Sign

Two parallel yellow lines with multiple pairs of double 
vertical yellow stripes. The letters "INST" are co-located with 
this hold line. These lines are located on taxiway F & G and 
are used to identify/protect the runway instrument landing 
systems. **NEVER CROSS THESE LINES WITHOUT CONTROL 
TOWER APPROVAL.**{.important} CMA markings are co-located with the 
"INST" marking/sign.

## Airfield Signs

![]\(../../files/imgs/training_slides_page_15_1.png)

### Taxiway Direction Sign
Taxiway direction signs have a **yellow background and 
black characters**, which identifies the designation or 
intersecting taxiways. Arrows indicate the direction of 
turn. Direction signs are normally located on the left 
side of the taxiway and prior to the intersection.

### Taxiway Location Sign
Taxiway location signs have a **black background 
and yellow characters**, which identifies the 
taxiway on which an aircraft or vehicle operator is 
located.

## Airfield Markings

### Circle of Safety 

The area surrounding the aircraft that vehicles may not normally penetrate. This area extends 10ft beyond the nose, 
tail, and wing of an of an aircraft. Green markings are segments that have been painted around the C-130 parking 
spots in the restricted area, which represents the circle of safety. Only essential vehicles may penetrate this circle. 

## Restricted Area

### Restricted Area Marking

![]\(../../files/imgs/training_slides_page_17_1.png)

A six inch wide red line around the C-130 parking 
apron used to delineate airfield restricted areas. 
**NEVER ENTER THESE AREAS UNLESS ESCORTED BY A 
AUTHORIZED INDIVIDUAL (RAB badge).**{.important}

### Entry Control Point (ECP)

![]\(../../files/imgs/training_slides_page_17_2.png)

ECP markings are 6" wide by 3' long retroreflective 
white bars separated by 6" gaps. **ALL PERSONNEL 
ENTERING A RESTRICTED AREA MUST HAVE A VALID 
RESTRICTED AREA BADGE**{.important} (AF Form 1199) authorizing 
access into the area or be escorted. 

## Operating Within the CMA
* **NO VEHICLE OPERATOR OR PEDESTRAIN SHALL ENTER THE CMA 
WITHOUT SPECIFIC APPROVAL FROM ATC TOWER.**{.important} 
* Vehicles must establish and maintain 2-way communication with 
the ATC Tower via the "Tower" net on a LMR prior to entering the 
CMA. 

    NOTE: It is prohibited from gaining approval to the CMA through a secondary agency. (Calling MOCC to 
call the ATC Tower to ask for permission) 

* Vehicles operators on the CMA must use a distinct approved call 
signs coordinated by the Wing ADPM, 19 CS, and published in 
LRAFBI 13-213.
* Vehicles operators must use light emitting diode/rotating beacon 
lights and/or emergency/hazard warning flashers when driving in 
the CMA.

## Tower Communication
* When operating vehicle on the airfield always maintain 2 way 
radio communication with tower at all times.
* When talking to tower use the following format: who your 
trying to contact, who you are, where you are, and state your 
intentions.
* Wait for the ATC tower to respond to your request. Read back 
their instructions verbatim, if you are unsure or the 
transmission was difficult to hear ask them to "say again". 
* **When Talking to Tower over the LMR NEVER say the words 
"Clear", "Cleared", or "Clearance".**{.important}

## Tower Communication
* Tower controls all aircraft, vehicle, and approved pedestrian traffic on the CMA by two-
way radio communications or, in the event of lost communications, by light gun signals. 
If use of light gun signals is unsuccessful the ATC Tower with raise and lower the 
intensity of the white Runway edge lights. All vehicles and pedestrians MUST exit the 
CMA.

## Construction 

![]\(../../files/imgs/training_slides_page_24_1.png)

When in the vicinity of a construction area stay clear of 
construction equipment (machines, vehicles, tools, etc.) Remain 
outside of the red and white barricades. If you notice any 
suspicions behaver or have any questions, please contact airfield 
operations.

## FOD Control/Prevention
* FOD checks are **EVERYONES**{.important} responsibility!
* Always ensure vehicle and tires are free of FOD before 
entering the airfield pavement or any airfield areas. 
* Make sure all vehicles have a FOD can.
* Make sure all equipment inside and outside of vehicle is  
safely secured and tied down before entering the airfield 
area. 

## Nighttime operation
* Vehicles operators must use light emitting 
diode/rotating beacon lights and/or emergency/hazard 
warning flashers when driving in the CMA.
* When approaching an aircraft at night, turn off the 
vehicle headlights and turn on the emergency flashers. 
Face the vehicle away from the aircraft.
