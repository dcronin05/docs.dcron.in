## Administrative

* **NCIC is operated under a “Shared Management” concept between the FBI and each authorized** **user**

* **FBI serves as the manager of the NCIC System**

* **NCIC is governed by the Advisory Policy Board**

* **Message will be generated for purged suppressed records.**
	* **Temporary felony warrant, purged after 48 hours**
	* **Felony vehicle records, purged after 90 days**
	* **Vehicles without a VIN/OAN, purged after 90 days**
	* **Boats without a BHN/OAN, purged after 90 days**
	* **Located property, purged after 10 days, located wanted persons, purged after 5 days**

**completeness.**

## Criminal Justice Information Services

* **Provides Criminal Justice Agencies and Non-Criminal Justice Agencies with a minimum set of**
**security requirements for access to AFOSI CJIS Division systems/information and how to protect**
**Criminal Justice Information (CJI).**

* **Standards ensures continuity of information protection from the creation of the information**
**through dissemination of the information.**

* **CJIS security policy applies to all entities with access to, or who operate in support of, AFOSI**
**CJIS Division’s services and information. All agencies covered under this policy to ensure its**
**protection.**

* **CJI is a term used for all data needed to perform your duties that comes through the AFOSI**
**CJIS.**

* **CJI includes but is not limited to:**
	* **Identity History**
	* **Property**
	* **Case History**
	* **Incident History**

* **Criminal History Record Information (CHRI) is a subset to CJI and is sometimes referred to as**
**“restricted data”.**

* **The following files shall be protected as CHRI:**
	* **Gang**
	* **Terrorist**
	* **Convicted Persons on Supervised Release**
	* **Immigration Violator**
	* **National Sex Offender Registry**
	* **Historical Protection Order**
	* **Identity Theft**

## NCIC

* **National Crime Information Center (NCIC) can be considered as stored data.**

* **NCIC is a nationwide computerized information system established as a service to all criminal**
**justice agencies – local, state, and federal**

* **Computerized index of documented criminal justice information concerning crimes and criminals**
**of nationwide interest and a locator-type file**

* **Came online on January 27, 1967**

* **Accurate and timely documented criminal justice information**

* **Programs:**
	* **NCIC**
	* **Uniform Crime Reporting (UCR)**
	* **Integrated Automated Fingerprint Identification System (IAFIS)**

* **Shared management concept with each users agency**

## Security / Misuse Incidents

* **Security incident is a violation or possible violation of the technical aspects of the NCIC Policy**
**that threatens the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of State/FBI CJIS data.**

* **Data stored in NCIC is confidential and should be treated as such.**

* **Users should activate password protected screen saver programs or lock the workstation when**
**they temporarily step away from the computer**

* **All queries must be “For Official Use Only.”**
	* **Misuse of NCIC Database could result loss of system access or disciplinary action taken by CC**
		* **Misuse includes running queries on:**
		* **Yourself**
		* **Co-workers (unless directed for an official investigation)**
		* **Family members**
		* **Chaplains**
		* **Child care providers**
		* **Sexual Assault Response Coordinators (SARC)**
		* **Neighbors**
		* **Victim Advocates**

* **Purpose Codes – Used while pulling a complete criminal history file. Remember we are**
**inquiry only and will only use purpose code “C”.**

|*Code*|*Explanation*
|---|---|
|C|Must be used when the IQ, FQ, or AQ is for official duties in connection with the administration of criminal justice.|
|E|Must be used for employment and licensing.|
|J|Must be used when the IQ, FQ, or AQ involves employment with a criminal justice agency or the screening of employees of other agencies or which the criminal justice agency is required to have management control. Criminal justice employment has been separated from other criminal justice purposes due to the requirement of some state agencies.|
|F|Must be used by criminal justice agencies in all states for screening applications for firearms and related permits. This includes firearms dealers, firearms purchasers, carriers of concealed weapons, explosive dealers and users, and lethal weapons dealers and users, but only when a Federal, state or local law/ordinance exists making the criminal justice agency responsible for the issuance of the licenses/permits.|
|D|Must be used by courts when hearing civil domestic violence or stalking cases. This purpose code shall not allow access to State sealed records.|
|S|Must be used by Defense Investigative Service and any other SCIA agencies authorized specifically by NLETS to access CHRI through the network using IQ, FQ, and AQ.|
|X|For use in conducting checks involving the emergency placement of children when unaccompanied by the immediate submission of fingerprints on the surrogate care provider(s).|

## Transaction Information

* **Using the Criminal History, NCIC, Index Inquiry (QH)(QHI) form, a QH/QHI**
**message is an inquiry to ascertain if a person's record is indexed in III.**

* **All III inquiries must contain a purpose code (C or J), and an attention, ATN,**
**Field.**

* **C is used during criminal investigations, while J is used for criminal justice**
**employment purposes only.**

* **Using the Criminal History, NCIC, Record Inquiry (QR) form, a QR message is a request for the**
**specific criminal history record that has been identified from the QH/QHI response.**

* **The FBI will respond with any Federal arrest record in III and with arrest records from non**
**participating states.**

* **A No record response indicated in III does not eliminate the possibility of a person having a**
**criminal history record on a local, state, or Federal level**.

* **NOTE:** **All required fields are in BLUE.**

### Hit Confirmation

* **Units should have an applicable QRC for Hit Confirmation procedures.**

* **Any agency receiving a record in response to an NCIC inquiry must confirm the “Hit” on the**
**record prior to arresting/apprehending the wanted person, taking the missing person into**
**custody, or seizing the stolen property .**
	* **NOTE: Detaining persons for officer safety until the “Hit” is confirmed is acceptable.**

* **BDOC Controller contacts the agency listed in the response to confirm the “Hit”.**
	* **For continuity reasons BDOC should be the Primary Contact for all Hit Confirmations.**

* **Confirming a “Hit” means to contact the agency that entered the record to ensure the person**
**or property inquired upon is identical to the person or property identified in the record.**
	* **Ensure the warrant, missing person report, or theft report is still outstanding.**
	* **Obtain the following information regarding: (1) the extradition of a wanted person when applicable, (2) the**
	**return of a missing person to the appropriate authorities, (3) the return of stolen property to its rightful owner,**
	**or (4) the terms and conditions of a protection order.**
	* **Nlets is the recommended network for “hit” confirmation even if the initial confirmation is handled via**
	**telephone.**

* **NLETS has created inquiry (YQ) and response (YR) formats for Hit confirmation. The (YQ)** **must contain one of the following priorities:**
	* **Priority 1/Urgent - confirm the hit within 10 minutes. This priority should be used in those instances where the Hit is the only basis for detaining a suspect or the nature of a case requires urgent confirmation of a hit.**
	* **Priority 2/Routine - confirm the hit within 1 hour. This priority will be used when the person is being held on**
	**local charges or when property has been located under circumstances where immediate action is not necessary.**
	**NOTE: After confirming the hit with the entering agency and upon taking a person into custody or acquiring**
	**property, the recovering agency must place a “locate” on the corresponding NCIC record. This will indicate to**
	**initiating law enforcement agency the subject or property location.**

* **Once the “hit” is confirmed, the BDOC Controller will direct the patrolman to “secure their**
**mics”. This warning will advise the patrolman to turn their radio down so it does not alert**
**the subject and prompt flight.**

* **BDOC Controller will dispatch another patrol to respond Code 2 to assist. Follow applicable**
**QRC and local SOP.**

* **Hit Confirmation**
	* **A positive response in which the person or property inquired upon matches the person or**
	**property contained in the response is referred to as a “hit”**
	* **Must be available 24 hours**
	* **URGENT – furnish record confirmation within 10 minutes**
	* **ROUTINE – furnish record confirmation within 1 hour**
	* **No response – send message to CTA**

* **Standards**
	* **Hit confirmation available for 24 hours**
	* **Timely entry and removal of record**
	* **Reasonable response time**
	* **Services availability**
* **Sanctions**
	* **Purge an agency’s records**
	* **Discontinuance of system access**
* **Reasons:**
	* **Failure to react properly to error notices**
	* **Failure to react properly to hit confirmation requests**
	* **Failure to locate**
	* **Failure to complete entries/modifications/removals promptly**
	* **Failure to make complete entries**
	* **Failure to validate**

## Terrorist Screening Center (TSC)

* **Although this is not a background check, it will provide feedback on whether the**
**person is wanted by any federal agency, to include the national Terrorist Screening**
**Center (TSC) IAW AFI 31-113.**

* **A positive hit, via the TSC, will indicate whether the person is included in the**
**national Terrorist Screen Center list.**

* **Information from the TSC can be found on the Security Force SMARTNet at**
**https://usaf.dps.mil/teams/SMARTNet/SitePages/S3P-Law-and-Order.aspx**

* **Immediately contact local AFOSI on all TSC “hits”. AFOSI will coordinate with**
**local FBI JTTF on plan of action.**
