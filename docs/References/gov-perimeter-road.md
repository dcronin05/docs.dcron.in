# GOV Use on Perimeter Road

## Memo 6 - Government Owned Vehicle (GOV) Use on Perimeter Road 

1. This memorandum outlines the use of 19 SFS GOVs on Perimeter Road, which is defined as the dirt/unimproved track surrounding the perimeter of the installation. This policy is established in an effort to mitigate vehicle damage, safeguard vehicle operators from abuse claims, and preserve vehicles for continued operations. 

2. Vehicles designed for use on improved roadways (i.e. sedans, pick-up trucks, and vans) will not be used for perimeter checks on Perimeter Road unless directly authorized by the VCO, Operations Staff (Ops Superintendent minimum) or the Command Team (CC, SEL). This includes pick-up trucks with four-wheel drive. 

	1. The only authorized vehicles to conduct perimeter patrols during normal circumstances are the Polaris side-by-sides, and the Polaris MRZR if certified to operate it.

	2. If no authorized vehicles are available, Flight Chiefs will contact the VCO and Operations Staff for authorization/guidance prior to conducting a perimeter check in a GOV, under non-emergency circumstances. 

3. In the event of an emergency, or when responding to a credible threat/incident along the perimeter, the on-duty Flight Chief may authorize the use of any GOV along Perimeter Road. Fault for any damage to an unauthorized vehicle operating on the Perimeter Road under such circumstances will be determined via an investigation. If appropriate, the operator will be held accountable for all damage/repair costs. Flight Chiefs are ultimately responsible for and entrusted to make scenario-based decisions while on duty; this responsibility shall not be hindered by this policy. 

4. Contact the Vehicle Control Officer (VCO) or S4V at DSN: 731-7075 with any questions or concerns regarding this memorandum.
