# Physical Training

## Memorandum 10: Physical Training Policy - Updated Oct 25

1. This memorandum outlines the updated 19 SFS Physical Training (PT) Policy and expectations for all personnel.
2. **All personnel assigned to the 19 SFS, permanently or temporarily, will conduct physical training every duty day.** Personnel will follow the schedules listed below depending on their assigned sections.
3. Physical training, and passing your required fitness/measurement tests, is and will remain to be a personal responsibility; the intent of these sessions is not to pass PT tests. While these sessions will help overall fitness, the intent is to: 1) grow our physical/tactical proficiency; 2) lead from the front; and 3) build team cohesion. To achieve that, workouts must be difficult, mission focused, and consistent. Expect these sessions to progress to focus on Security Forces-specific drills, tactics, workouts, and team building challenges. PT outlines will be maintained by 19 SFS/S1 and distributed bi-weekly with workouts/activities for Staff and Flight.
4. STAFF PERSONNEL: Staff personnel are defined as those members assigned to S1, S2, S3, S4, and S5 that are not shift workers, including VCC, CATM, MWD Staff, and Training. Staff personnel generally work M-F, 0800 1700. Staff personnel will attend Staff-PT IAW the below schedule:

| --------- | ---- | --------------------------- |
| Tuesday   | 0800 | Strength                    |
| Wednesday | 0800 | Personal PT/Recovery/Sports |
| Thursday  | 0800 | Endurance                   |

5. FLIGHT PERSONNEL: Flight personnel are those 19 SFS members whose primary duties are shift work within S3 (Alpha, Bravo, Charlie, Delta, MWD Flights). Flight personnel generally work a Panama 12 schedule. Flight PT will be at the time/location directed by the Flight Chief. **Flight Chiefs should make every effort to facilitate personal/Flight-PT time during the duty day.** The Flight program is broken down to incorporate Flight-PT on the final day of each shift, and during the Flight Training Day. 6. Physical Training during Squadron PT sessions (Staff-/Flight-PT, not personal PT days) will normally be conducted in uniform. The default uniform will be the PT Uniform; however, some sessions may be conducted in OCPs or civilian attire. The default location is the 19 SFS Vehicle Bay; however, sessions may change locations to incorporate diverse equipment/terrain as needed.

7. 19 SFS members will attend scheduled PT sessions. Failure to attend PT sessions (including failure to provide proof of personal PT) will be handled IAW the Squadron's missed appointment policy. SFS members who require continued/long-term/unique accommodation (profile, appointments, etc.) will develop individual PT policies that will be signed by the member, supervisor, and 19 SFS/S1 and recorded in the member's PIF. SFS members are encouraged to develop personal physical training plans to supplement Staff-/Flight-PT. Profiles and PT limitations will be handled on a case-by-case basis. Security Forces is an extremely physical career field and requires dedicated rest/recovery/foundation days to ensure continued mission effectiveness. PT is a readiness tool to ensure we are prepared for the next fight; it should not contribute to a degradation in readiness. However, PT should be challenging and beneficial - there is a balance we aim to achieve with the 19 SFS PT Program.

8. 19 SFS/S1, 2d Lt Chavez will be the POC for the PT program. 19 SFS/S3T will help to build, lead, and vector the PT program to meet Squadron training goals. They are open to and encourage feedback on continuing to build an effective PT program.
